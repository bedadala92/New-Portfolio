Background:

Drapermeter is a web application that lets participants rate advertisements based on their creativity. This project was done as part of my Masters Thesis at the University of Florida. Drapermeter verifies an existing theory in Advertising Creativity known as the Creative Product Semantic Scale (CPSS). According to CPSS, any product or service can be deemed creative if it scores highly on the following three scales :
1. Novelty
2. Elaboration
3. Style
To understand the influence of creativity on the effectiveness of an advertisement and the differences between the judgement of creativity by general consumers and creative professionals is studied through an online survey tool that asks participants to rate award winning advertisements based on the above three scales. These results are then undergone some statistical analysis to see if there is a correlation between the above scales and the overall creativity of an advertisement.

Research:

Surveys that provide good user experience are proven to obtain better quality of data than typical surveys that fail to engage participants. To better understand the current trends in survey design, three popular survey applications were analyzed. Following are the major differences between each:

This survey will have 27 questions in total with 9 questions for each advertisement. Building a survey of 27 questions using any of the above three apps results in a long vertical navigation or 27 repeated clicks. Another important consideration is that user should see the advertisement before answering a question and none of the above mentioned survey platforms provide this feature. Therefore, I decided to build a custom survey application for this research that fits the needs and almost gamifies the survey experience.

UX Design process:

Based on the research paper "Gamification of online surveys: conceptual foundations and a design process based on the MDA framework", following 5 steps are devised for the development of an interactive survey:
1. Inspiration
2. Aesthetics and Relationships
3. Dynamics and Conversations
4. Mechanics and Appearance
5. Prototyping, Evaluation and Iteration

1. Inspiration:

At this stage, existing work in survey design that deviates from the mainstream in terms of UI elements and the overall user experience are studied. Here are some of them:
Made in a free world: Slavery Footprint Survey
New York Times: How Y’all, Youse and You Guys Talk
New York Times: How Many Households Are Like Yours?
In my observations, following elements align with the concepts discussed in the research that are providing a compelling survey experience:
- Providing visual feedback for an input
- Using subtle animations during transitions
- Informing the participant about background of the survey
- Usage of sliders and other interactive elements for data input

2. Aesthetics and Relationships:

User personas are developed through interviews to better understand the goals and pain points of the survey participant. From the personas it is understood that millennial consumers lack the motivation to participate in a survey and this is largely due to the preconceived notion of doing surveys as a mundane task. 3. Dynamics and Conversations

Dynamics are the conditions under which we want the user to complete the survey. Examples of dynamics include time i.e, a timed survey, limited characters for a response etc., Choosing a dynamic depends on the goal of the project. Through Drapermeter, we are trying to see patterns in people's perception of creativity, hence the data collected through this survey should be numerical and ordinal. Likert Scale Questionnaires are the standard way of collecting ordinal data. Input for likert scale questionnaire can either be through radio buttons or sliders. In most of the popular survey applications radio buttons are used for this kind of responses.

4. Mechanics and Appearance

After finalizing the information architecture, wireframes are developed for each page and these are later used to develop high-fidelity mockups. The entire visual design of this project is inspired by the iconography and color-palette of the TV show Mad Men.

This app is setup using the MEAN stack (MongoDB, Express Js, Angular Js, Node Js) and deployed through Heroku. Other libraries used in the development include Bootstrap, jQuery and SVG.

5.Prototyping, Evaluation and Iteration

For the survey page, two different layouts, one with radio buttons and the other with sliders are developed. A simple A/B test is conducted with three participation to test for optimization. From the A/B tests it is learned that the layout with sliders has significant difference in ease of use and the time taken to complete the survey.

Based on these results, Layout B (sliders) was used in the final survey application.
